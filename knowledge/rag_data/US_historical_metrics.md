# US Equity Market Historical Performance & Metrics

## Overview

The US equity market has delivered strong long-term returns with moderate volatility, making it one of the most attractive equity markets globally. Historical data reveals consistent outperformance over multiple decades, though with periodic significant drawdowns and volatility episodes.

## Long-Term Performance Metrics

### 30-Year Performance (1994-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~10.0% (S&P 500)
- **Volatility**: ~15-16% annualized
- **Max Drawdown**: ~50% (2008-2009 financial crisis)
- **Best Year**: +37.6% (1995)
- **Worst Year**: -37.0% (2008)

### 20-Year Performance (2004-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~9.5% (S&P 500)
- **Volatility**: ~16% annualized
- **Max Drawdown**: ~50% (2008-2009)
- **Recovery Period**: ~4 years to recover from 2008 peak

### 10-Year Performance (2014-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~12-13% (S&P 500)
- **Volatility**: ~18% annualized (elevated due to COVID-19)
- **Max Drawdown**: ~34% (COVID-19 crash, March 2020)
- **Recovery Period**: ~5 months (remarkably fast)

### 5-Year Performance (2019-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~11-12% (S&P 500)
- **Volatility**: ~20% annualized (high due to COVID-19 and 2022 bear market)
- **Max Drawdown**: ~25% (2022 bear market)

## Volatility Analysis

### Historical Volatility Ranges

- **Low Volatility Periods** (10-12%): 2017, mid-2000s, late 1990s
- **Normal Volatility** (15-18%): Typical long-term average
- **High Volatility Periods** (20-30%): 2008-2009, 2020, 2022

### Volatility Interpretation

- **15-16% annualized volatility** indicates moderate uncertainty, suitable for long-term investors with risk tolerance
- **Volatility clustering**: High volatility periods tend to cluster (e.g., 2008-2009, 2020-2022)
- **Volatility compression**: Extended low-volatility periods (e.g., 2017) often precede volatility spikes

## Drawdown Analysis

### Major Drawdowns (Post-1990)

1. **2008-2009 Financial Crisis**: -50% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~17 months
   - Recovery: ~4 years
   - Cause: Financial system collapse, credit crisis

2. **2000-2002 Dot-Com Crash**: -49% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~31 months
   - Recovery: ~5 years
   - Cause: Technology bubble, overvaluation

3. **2020 COVID-19 Crash**: -34% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~1 month (fastest crash in history)
   - Recovery: ~5 months
   - Cause: Pandemic-induced economic shutdown

4. **2022 Bear Market**: -25% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~10 months
   - Recovery: ~1 year
   - Cause: Inflation, Fed tightening, valuation concerns

### Drawdown Interpretation

- **Frequency**: Major drawdowns (>20%) occur approximately every 5-7 years
- **Recovery Time**: Typically 1-4 years to recover to previous peaks
- **Severity**: Drawdowns of 30-50% are not uncommon but are followed by strong recoveries

## Return Distribution Characteristics

### Positive vs Negative Years

- **Positive Years**: ~75% of years are positive (3 out of 4 years)
- **Average Positive Year**: +20-25%
- **Average Negative Year**: -12-15%
- **Consecutive Negative Years**: Rare (typically 1-2 years maximum)

### Return Persistence

- **Momentum Effects**: Strong years often followed by strong years (momentum)
- **Mean Reversion**: After major drawdowns, strong recoveries typically follow
- **Long-Term Trend**: Consistent upward trend over 30+ year periods

## Risk-Adjusted Returns

### Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Free Rate Assumed 3%)

- **30-Year Sharpe**: ~0.45-0.50 (moderate risk-adjusted returns)
- **10-Year Sharpe**: ~0.50-0.60 (improved in recent decade)
- **Interpretation**: Positive risk-adjusted returns indicate compensation for volatility

### Maximum Adverse Excursion

- **Typical Intra-Year Drawdown**: 10-15% even in positive years
- **Volatility Drag**: High volatility reduces compound returns relative to arithmetic average
- **Recovery Patterns**: Markets typically recover from intra-year drawdowns within the same year

## Investment Horizon Implications

### Short-Term (1-3 Years)

- **High Uncertainty**: Returns can range from -30% to +40% annually
- **Volatility Dominant**: Short-term returns driven by volatility, not fundamentals
- **Timing Risk**: Entry/exit timing significantly impacts returns

### Medium-Term (5-10 Years)

- **Reduced Uncertainty**: Returns typically range from -5% to +15% annualized
- **Fundamental Drivers**: Economic cycles and earnings growth become more relevant
- **Compounding Effects**: Positive returns compound, negative returns recover

### Long-Term (10+ Years)

- **Lower Uncertainty**: Returns typically converge toward 8-12% annualized
- **Fundamental Dominant**: Long-term returns driven by economic growth and earnings
- **Volatility Impact Reduced**: Short-term volatility has less impact on long-term outcomes

## Common Questions

**Q: What does 10% annualized return mean over 30 years?**
A: A 10% annualized return means that $100 invested would grow to approximately $1,745 over 30 years (before inflation). However, the actual path includes significant volatility - some years up 30%+, others down 30%+. The annualized figure smooths out this volatility to show the average compound growth rate.

**Q: How should I interpret 15-16% volatility?**
A: 15-16% annualized volatility means that in a typical year, the market might move up or down by 15-16% from its average. In practice, this means roughly 2 out of 3 years will see returns within -15% to +15% of the average, while 1 out of 3 years will see larger moves. This level of volatility requires risk tolerance and a long-term investment horizon.

**Q: Why did the market recover so quickly from COVID-19 but slowly from 2008?**
A: The 2020 COVID-19 crash was driven by an external shock (pandemic) with massive fiscal and monetary policy response ($5+ trillion stimulus, zero interest rates). The 2008 crisis was a structural financial system collapse requiring fundamental repair. Policy response speed and market structure differences explain the recovery speed difference.
