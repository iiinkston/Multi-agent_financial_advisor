# UK Equity Market Historical Performance & Metrics

## Overview

The UK equity market (FTSE 100) has delivered moderate long-term returns with higher volatility than US markets, reflecting commodity exposure, currency sensitivity, and post-Brexit structural changes. The market is characterized by high dividend yields and value-oriented characteristics.

## Long-Term Performance Metrics

### 30-Year Performance (1994-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~7-8% (FTSE 100)
- **Volatility**: ~17-18% annualized
- **Max Drawdown**: ~45% (2008-2009 financial crisis)
- **Best Year**: +33.1% (1997)
- **Worst Year**: -31.3% (2008)

### 20-Year Performance (2004-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~6-7% (FTSE 100)
- **Volatility**: ~18% annualized
- **Max Drawdown**: ~45% (2008-2009)
- **Recovery Period**: ~5 years to recover from 2008 peak

### 10-Year Performance (2014-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~5-6% (FTSE 100, lower than US)
- **Volatility**: ~18-20% annualized
- **Max Drawdown**: ~35% (COVID-19 crash, March 2020)
- **Brexit Impact**: Underperformance relative to global markets post-2016

## Volatility Analysis

### Historical Volatility Ranges

- **Low Volatility Periods** (12-14%): Mid-2000s, 2017
- **Normal Volatility** (17-18%): Typical long-term average
- **High Volatility Periods** (20-25%): 2008-2009, 2020, Brexit-related periods

### Volatility Interpretation

- **17-18% annualized volatility** indicates higher uncertainty than US market, reflecting commodity exposure and currency sensitivity
- **Currency Impact**: GBP volatility adds to equity volatility for foreign investors
- **Commodity Sensitivity**: Energy and mining sectors create additional volatility

## Drawdown Analysis

### Major Drawdowns (Post-1990)

1. **2008-2009 Financial Crisis**: -45% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~17 months
   - Recovery: ~5 years
   - Cause: Financial system collapse, UK bank exposure

2. **2000-2002 Dot-Com Crash**: -40% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~31 months
   - Recovery: ~4 years

3. **2020 COVID-19 Crash**: -35% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~1 month
   - Recovery: ~6 months

4. **2016 Brexit Vote**: -15% immediate reaction, longer-term underperformance

### Drawdown Interpretation

- **Frequency**: Major drawdowns occur approximately every 5-7 years
- **Recovery Time**: Typically 4-5 years, longer than US market
- **Brexit Impact**: Post-2016 underperformance relative to global peers

## Return Distribution Characteristics

### Positive vs Negative Years

- **Positive Years**: ~70% of years are positive (slightly lower than US)
- **Average Positive Year**: +18-20%
- **Average Negative Year**: -14-16%
- **Dividend Contribution**: ~3-4% annual dividend yield supports total returns

### Return Persistence

- **Value Characteristics**: Market tends toward value, less momentum-driven than US
- **Currency Effects**: GBP strength/weakness affects returns for foreign investors
- **Commodity Cycles**: Energy and mining sectors create cyclical return patterns

## Risk-Adjusted Returns

### Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Free Rate Assumed 3%)

- **30-Year Sharpe**: ~0.25-0.30 (lower than US, reflecting lower returns and higher volatility)
- **10-Year Sharpe**: ~0.15-0.20 (post-Brexit underperformance)
- **Interpretation**: Lower risk-adjusted returns reflect structural challenges and commodity exposure

## Dividend Characteristics

### Dividend Yield

- **Historical Average**: ~3.5-4.0% (significantly higher than US ~1.5-2.0%)
- **Dividend Growth**: Modest growth, ~2-3% annually
- **Dividend Sustainability**: Generally strong, though some sectors (energy) more volatile

### Total Return vs Price Return

- **Price Return**: ~4-5% annualized (lower than US)
- **Total Return (with dividends)**: ~7-8% annualized
- **Dividend Contribution**: Dividends represent ~40-50% of total returns

## Investment Horizon Implications

### Short-Term (1-3 Years)

- **High Uncertainty**: Returns can range from -30% to +35% annually
- **Currency Risk**: GBP volatility adds uncertainty for foreign investors
- **Brexit Sensitivity**: Policy and trade relationship changes create volatility

### Medium-Term (5-10 Years)

- **Moderate Returns**: Returns typically range from 0% to +10% annualized
- **Dividend Support**: High dividend yields provide income cushion
- **Structural Challenges**: Post-Brexit adjustment period affects performance

### Long-Term (10+ Years)

- **Lower Returns**: Returns typically converge toward 6-8% annualized (lower than US)
- **Dividend Focus**: Income generation becomes more important component
- **Value Characteristics**: Market tends to trade at lower valuations than US

## Common Questions

**Q: Why does the UK market underperform the US market?**
A: UK market underperformance reflects: (1) Lower technology sector representation, (2) Commodity sector exposure creating cyclical volatility, (3) Post-Brexit structural challenges and trade disruption, (4) Currency effects (GBP weakness), and (5) Value-oriented characteristics with lower growth rates. However, higher dividend yields partially offset lower price appreciation.

**Q: How do dividends affect UK market returns?**
A: UK market dividends (~3.5-4% yield) represent ~40-50% of total returns over long periods. This income component provides stability and supports total returns even when price appreciation is modest. For income-focused investors, UK market's high dividend yield is a key attraction.

**Q: What is the impact of Brexit on UK market performance?**
A: Post-2016 Brexit vote, UK market has underperformed global peers due to: (1) Trade disruption and reduced EU market access, (2) Currency volatility (GBP weakness), (3) Regulatory uncertainty and policy changes, (4) Reduced foreign investment flows, and (5) Structural adjustment period. Recovery depends on new trade relationships and policy stability.
