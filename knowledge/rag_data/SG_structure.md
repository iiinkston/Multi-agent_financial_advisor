# Singapore Equity Market Structure

## Overview

Singapore's equity market serves as a regional financial hub for Southeast Asia, characterized by strong banking sector, real estate investment trusts (REITs), and government-linked companies (GLCs). The market is relatively small but highly liquid and well-regulated, attracting regional and international investors.

## Market Capitalization and Index Construction

### Primary Indices

- **Straits Times Index (STI)**: Market-capitalization weighted index of 30 largest Singapore-listed companies
- **FTSE ST All-Share**: Comprehensive index covering all listed companies
- **Sector Indices**: Banking, REIT, and industrial sub-indices

### Market Capitalization Characteristics

- Total Singapore equity market capitalization: ~S$800 billion (as of 2024)
- Large-cap (>S$1B): ~70% of market cap
- Mid-cap (S$200M-S$1B): ~25% of market cap
- Small-cap (<S$200M): ~5% of market cap

## Macro Drivers

### Monetary Policy

The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) manages policy through exchange rate targeting:

- **Exchange Rate Policy**: MAS manages SGD against a trade-weighted basket, not interest rates directly
- **Currency Appreciation**: Gradual SGD appreciation supports purchasing power but affects export competitiveness
- **Foreign Reserves**: Large reserves provide stability and confidence

### Fiscal Policy

- **Government Investment**: Temasek Holdings and GIC (sovereign wealth funds) are major market participants
- **Tax Policy**: Low corporate tax rates (17%) and no capital gains tax attract international companies
- **Infrastructure Investment**: Government infrastructure spending supports construction and industrial sectors

### Economic Indicators

Key macroeconomic factors:

- **GDP Growth**: Mature economy with growth typically 2-4% annually, driven by services and finance
- **Unemployment**: Very low (~2-3%), reflecting tight labor market
- **Inflation**: Generally low and stable (~1-2%), though influenced by imported goods
- **Trade**: High trade-to-GDP ratio (~300%), making economy sensitive to global trade cycles

## Sector Composition

The Singapore market is dominated by:

1. **Financials** (~35% of STI): Three major banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB) dominate, strong regional presence
2. **Real Estate/REITs** (~20%): Large REIT sector with ~40 listed REITs, high dividend yields
3. **Telecommunications** (~10%): Singtel with regional operations
4. **Industrials** (~8%): Keppel, Sembcorp, marine and offshore engineering
5. **Consumer** (~7%): Retail, food & beverage, regional consumer brands

See `SG_sector_composition.md` for detailed sector analysis.

## Market Liquidity Characteristics

- **Average Daily Trading Volume**: ~S$1-1.5 billion
- **Bid-Ask Spreads**: Typically 10-20 basis points for large-cap stocks
- **Market Depth**: Good liquidity for STI constituents, moderate for mid-caps
- **Trading Venues**: Singapore Exchange (SGX) primary venue

## Investor Behavior Patterns

### Institutional Participation

- **Institutional Ownership**: ~70% of market cap, including significant GLC ownership
- **Foreign Ownership**: ~40% of market owned by international investors
- **Government-Linked Companies**: Temasek Holdings and GIC are major shareholders in many listed companies
- **Regional Focus**: Strong participation from Southeast Asian institutional investors

### Retail Participation

- **Direct Ownership**: ~30% of market cap
- **CPF Investment**: Central Provident Fund allows Singaporeans to invest retirement savings in equities
- **Dividend Preference**: High dividend yields attract retail income investors

## Regulatory Framework

- **Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)**: Primary regulator for financial markets and institutions
- **Singapore Exchange (SGX)**: Market operator and regulator for listed companies
- **Corporate Governance**: Strong governance standards, with Code of Corporate Governance
- **Disclosure Requirements**: Comprehensive continuous disclosure obligations

## Market Access and Trading

- **Trading Hours**: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM SGT (regular hours)
- **Settlement**: T+2 settlement cycle
- **Currency**: SGD-denominated
- **Derivatives**: SGX provides extensive derivatives including equity index futures and options

## Key Structural Characteristics

### Strengths

- Strong banking sector with regional presence and profitability
- High-quality REIT sector with attractive yields (~5-6%)
- Stable regulatory environment and rule of law
- Strategic location as regional financial hub
- Government-linked companies provide stability

### Vulnerabilities

- Small market size limits diversification opportunities
- High concentration in financials and real estate
- Limited technology sector representation
- Sensitivity to regional economic cycles
- Currency policy constraints on monetary flexibility

## Government-Linked Companies (GLCs)

### Role and Impact

- **Temasek Holdings**: Investment company with significant stakes in major listed companies
- **GIC**: Sovereign wealth fund with global investments
- **Stability**: GLC ownership provides long-term stability and strategic direction
- **Governance**: High governance standards but potential conflicts with commercial objectives

### Major GLC Holdings

- Banking: DBS, OCBC, UOB (partial GLC ownership)
- Telecommunications: Singtel
- Industrials: Keppel, Sembcorp
- Real Estate: Various REITs and property developers

## REIT Market Characteristics

Singapore has one of Asia's largest REIT markets:

- **Number of REITs**: ~40 listed REITs
- **Total Market Cap**: ~S$100 billion
- **Average Yield**: 5-6%, attractive relative to global REIT markets
- **Property Types**: Retail, office, industrial, hospitality, healthcare
- **Regulatory Framework**: REIT Code provides tax transparency and distribution requirements

## Common Questions

**Q: Why is Singapore's market so banking-heavy?**
A: Singapore's three major banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB) are among the largest and most profitable in Southeast Asia, with strong regional operations. The financial services sector is a core pillar of Singapore's economy, supported by the country's role as a regional financial hub and strong regulatory framework.

**Q: How do government-linked companies affect market behavior?**
A: GLCs, through Temasek and GIC, provide stability and long-term orientation but can also create concentration risk and potential conflicts between commercial and strategic objectives. Their presence generally supports governance standards but may reduce market efficiency in some cases.

**Q: What makes Singapore's REIT market attractive?**
A: Singapore REITs offer high dividend yields (5-6%), tax transparency, strong regulatory framework, and exposure to stable property income streams. The sector benefits from Singapore's role as a regional hub and strong property fundamentals, making it attractive for income-focused investors.
