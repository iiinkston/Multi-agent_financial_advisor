# Hong Kong Equity Market Historical Performance & Metrics

## Overview

Hong Kong's equity market (Hang Seng Index) has delivered moderate long-term returns with high volatility, reflecting its role as gateway to China, sensitivity to mainland policy, and exposure to global risk sentiment. The market is characterized by high correlation with mainland China and significant technology sector representation.

## Long-Term Performance Metrics

### 20-Year Performance (2004-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~6-7% (HSI)
- **Volatility**: ~22-24% annualized (high)
- **Max Drawdown**: ~65% (2008-2009 financial crisis)
- **Best Year**: +52.0% (2009 recovery)
- **Worst Year**: -48.3% (2008)

### 10-Year Performance (2014-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~4-5% (HSI, lower than global markets)
- **Volatility**: ~24-26% annualized (very high)
- **Max Drawdown**: ~40% (multiple episodes: 2015, 2018, 2020, 2022)
- **Mainland Correlation**: High correlation with mainland China market performance

## Volatility Analysis

### Historical Volatility Ranges

- **Low Volatility Periods** (15-18%): Rare, typically during stable mainland policy periods
- **Normal Volatility** (22-24%): Typical long-term average (high)
- **High Volatility Periods** (30-40%): 2008-2009, 2015, 2018, 2020, 2022

### Volatility Interpretation

- **22-24% annualized volatility** indicates high uncertainty, reflecting mainland policy sensitivity and global risk sentiment
- **Policy-Driven Volatility**: Mainland policy changes (tech regulation, property policy) create significant volatility
- **Currency Peg Impact**: HK$ peg to USD creates interest rate sensitivity and capital flow volatility

## Drawdown Analysis

### Major Drawdowns (Post-2000)

1. **2008-2009 Financial Crisis**: -65% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~12 months
   - Recovery: ~3-4 years
   - Cause: Global financial crisis, high leverage, property exposure

2. **2015 China Market Crash**: -35% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~3 months
   - Recovery: ~2 years
   - Cause: Mainland A-share bubble, capital controls

3. **2018 Trade War**: -25% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~6 months
   - Recovery: ~1 year
   - Cause: US-China trade tensions, policy uncertainty

4. **2020 COVID-19 + Tech Regulation**: -40% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~6 months
   - Recovery: Ongoing, slower than global markets
   - Cause: Pandemic, mainland tech regulation, geopolitical tensions

5. **2022 Policy + Geopolitical**: -30% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~12 months
   - Recovery: Ongoing
   - Cause: Mainland policy tightening, property crisis, US-China tensions

### Drawdown Interpretation

- **Frequency**: Major drawdowns occur approximately every 2-3 years (more frequent than other markets)
- **Recovery Time**: Typically 1-3 years, but can be prolonged during policy uncertainty
- **Severity**: Drawdowns of 30-65% are common, reflecting high volatility and policy sensitivity

## Return Distribution Characteristics

### Positive vs Negative Years

- **Positive Years**: ~60-65% of years are positive (lower than developed markets)
- **Average Positive Year**: +25-30% (high when positive)
- **Average Negative Year**: -20-25% (severe when negative)
- **High Dispersion**: Large swings between best and worst years

### Return Persistence

- **Policy-Driven**: Returns highly sensitive to mainland policy changes
- **Momentum Effects**: Strong years often followed by strong years, but reversals are sharp
- **Geopolitical Sensitivity**: US-China tensions create volatility and capital flow disruptions

## Risk-Adjusted Returns

### Sharpe Ratio (Risk-Free Rate Assumed 2%)

- **20-Year Sharpe**: ~0.20-0.25 (lower than developed markets, reflecting high volatility)
- **10-Year Sharpe**: ~0.15-0.20 (lower in recent decade due to policy volatility)
- **Interpretation**: Lower risk-adjusted returns reflect high volatility and policy-driven dynamics

## Mainland China Correlation

### Correlation Characteristics

- **High Correlation**: ~0.7-0.8 correlation with mainland A-share market
- **H-Share Dominance**: H-shares represent ~60% of market cap, driving correlation
- **Policy Transmission**: Mainland policy changes immediately affect Hong Kong market
- **Capital Flow Linkage**: Stock Connect programs increase correlation

### Impact on Performance

- **Mainland Growth**: Strong mainland growth supports Hong Kong market
- **Mainland Policy**: Policy changes (tech regulation, property policy) create volatility
- **Capital Controls**: Mainland capital controls affect Hong Kong market access and liquidity

## Investment Horizon Implications

### Short-Term (1-3 Years)

- **Very High Uncertainty**: Returns can range from -40% to +50% annually
- **Policy Risk**: Mainland policy changes can trigger immediate 20-30% moves
- **Geopolitical Risk**: US-China tensions create capital flow disruptions

### Medium-Term (5-10 Years)

- **Moderate Returns**: Returns typically range from -5% to +10% annualized
- **Policy Cycles**: Performance driven by mainland policy cycles and economic conditions
- **Structural Challenges**: Geopolitical tensions and policy uncertainty create headwinds

### Long-Term (10+ Years)

- **Moderate Returns**: Returns typically converge toward 5-7% annualized (if policy stabilizes)
- **Gateway Role**: Long-term value depends on maintaining gateway role to mainland China
- **Policy Dependency**: Performance highly dependent on mainland policy stability and US-China relations

## Common Questions

**Q: Why is Hong Kong market so volatile?**
A: Hong Kong market's high volatility (22-24% annualized) reflects: (1) High correlation with volatile mainland China market, (2) Policy-driven dynamics (mainland policy changes create immediate 20-30% moves), (3) Geopolitical sensitivity (US-China tensions), (4) Currency peg constraints, and (5) High leverage and property exposure. This volatility requires high risk tolerance.

**Q: How does mainland China policy affect Hong Kong market?**
A: Mainland policy has immediate and significant impact due to: (1) H-shares representing ~60% of market cap, (2) High correlation (~0.7-0.8) with A-share market, (3) Policy transmission through Stock Connect programs, and (4) Capital flow linkages. Policy changes (tech regulation, property policy, capital controls) can trigger 20-30% market moves within days or weeks.

**Q: What are the risks of investing in Hong Kong market?**
A: Key risks include: (1) Policy-driven volatility (mainland policy changes), (2) Geopolitical tensions (US-China relations), (3) High volatility (22-24% annualized), (4) Currency peg constraints, (5) Property and leverage exposure, and (6) Regulatory uncertainty with increasing mainland integration. These risks require high risk tolerance and policy monitoring.
