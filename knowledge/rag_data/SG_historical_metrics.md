# Singapore Equity Market Historical Performance & Metrics

## Overview

Singapore's equity market (Straits Times Index) has delivered moderate long-term returns with moderate volatility, reflecting the market's defensive characteristics, high dividend yields, and regional economic sensitivity. The market is characterized by stability, strong banking sector, and REIT income generation.

## Long-Term Performance Metrics

### 20-Year Performance (2004-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~6-7% (STI)
- **Volatility**: ~16-17% annualized
- **Max Drawdown**: ~50% (2008-2009 financial crisis)
- **Best Year**: +32.5% (2009 recovery)
- **Worst Year**: -42.0% (2008)

### 10-Year Performance (2014-2024)

- **Annualized Return**: ~4-5% (STI, lower than global markets)
- **Volatility**: ~17-18% annualized
- **Max Drawdown**: ~35% (COVID-19 crash, March 2020)
- **Regional Sensitivity**: Performance correlated with regional economic cycles

## Volatility Analysis

### Historical Volatility Ranges

- **Low Volatility Periods** (12-14%): 2017, mid-2000s
- **Normal Volatility** (16-17%): Typical long-term average
- **High Volatility Periods** (20-25%): 2008-2009, 2020, regional crisis periods

### Volatility Interpretation

- **16-17% annualized volatility** indicates moderate uncertainty, lower than many regional markets
- **Defensive Characteristics**: Lower volatility reflects defensive sector composition (banks, REITs, utilities)
- **Regional Correlation**: Volatility spikes during regional economic stress (Asian financial crisis, regional slowdowns)

## Drawdown Analysis

### Major Drawdowns (Post-2000)

1. **2008-2009 Financial Crisis**: -50% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~12 months
   - Recovery: ~3-4 years
   - Cause: Global financial crisis, regional impact

2. **2020 COVID-19 Crash**: -35% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~1 month
   - Recovery: ~8 months
   - Cause: Pandemic, regional economic shutdown

3. **2015-2016 Regional Slowdown**: -20% peak-to-trough
   - Duration: ~12 months
   - Recovery: ~2 years
   - Cause: China slowdown, regional economic weakness

### Drawdown Interpretation

- **Frequency**: Major drawdowns occur approximately every 5-7 years
- **Recovery Time**: Typically 2-4 years, moderate recovery speed
- **Defensive Nature**: Drawdowns typically less severe than growth markets but recovery can be slower

## Common Questions

**Q: Why does Singapore market have such high dividend yields?**
A: Singapore market's high dividend yield (~4-4.5%) reflects: (1) Large REIT sector with high distribution yields (5-6%), (2) Major banks with strong dividend policies, (3) Mature companies with stable cash flows, and (4) Tax-advantaged structures (REITs). This income focus makes the market attractive for income-seeking investors.

**Q: How does regional economic performance affect Singapore market?**
A: Singapore market is highly correlated with regional (Southeast Asia, China) economic cycles due to: (1) Trade-dependent economy (trade-to-GDP ~300%), (2) Regional banking operations, (3) Regional REIT property exposure, and (4) Regional supply chain integration. China slowdown or regional stress directly impacts Singapore market performance.

**Q: What are the defensive characteristics of Singapore market?**
A: Singapore market's defensive nature comes from: (1) Strong banking sector with regional profitability, (2) High-quality REIT sector with stable income, (3) Government-linked companies providing stability, (4) Mature economy with lower growth volatility, and (5) Strong regulatory framework. However, defensive characteristics also mean lower growth potential compared to growth markets.
